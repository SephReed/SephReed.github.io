Hexapod

About this build:

The Hexapod is a sphere like geodesic shape with the interior made of mirrors. It stands at an inch short of 10 feet, has a max diameter of 12 feet, and is currently being submitted for the SXSW art grant.

About the builders:

Leland Drexler-Russell launched TransPlant Design to imbue the world with an added sense of enchantment. He use special effects techniques to create large scale interactive installations that envelop participants in surreal environs. His inspiration comes from the amazingly diverse flora and fauna found on our earth and speculative astrobiology of faraway worlds. Though he come from an architectural background, he has found creating intriguing experiences to be far more rewarding.

Dominique Davis is a Projection artist and video technician. The Focus of his work is connecting people to their environment through interactive art and technology. His works include installations built for Austin Energy, UT neuroscience graduate program, and international video projection works in Zurich, Switzerland and Berlin, Germany.

Seph Reed (legal name: Scott Jaromin) is an all around builder and coder. Having started on computers as a toddler, he began programming at age 10 and 3d modelling at 14, and has since moved into the world of fabrication with a focus on immersion and interactivity. He was the build lead for the highly regarded Vessel by Vision Gland, has worked on large scale metal sculptures for Burning Man, and likes to spend his free time programming with experimental web technology.

Misty Nickle is an experiential artist whose work centralizes on creating community and play. She is a core member of the Iron Monkey Arts, a group which has received multiple honoraria grants to build large interactive metal sculptures for Burning Man, Critical Northwest, and Seacompression. Her most recent work for the 2016 East Austin Studio Tours with Vision Gland was an immersive installation for people of all ages, which consisted of secret passageways and a slide into a ball pit.
